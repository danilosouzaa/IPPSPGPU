  • This neighborhood is similar to Change One Mode (COM), but its purpose is change two execution modes m1 and m2, which corresponds to two jobs j1 and j2. Similarly, this neighborhood verifies if the changes are valid or not in relation to non-renewable resources.
         To perform this process, it is necessary to receive as a parameter a vector M of execution modes for all jobs, the jobs j1 and j2 and the new modes m1 and m2.
         Figure 12 shows the movement made by this neighborhood. In this example, the method receives as parameter jobs 1, 2 and the new modes 0, 1. Thus, the current execution modes 1, 2 of these jobs are changed to 0, 1.
