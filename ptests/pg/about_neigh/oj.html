  • This neighborhood is somewhat similar to neighborhood Offset Project (OP), but its purpose is to perform a displacement of size k in just one job j.
         To perform this process, it is necessary to receive as a parameter the allocations sequence S, the index i of job j to be displaced, the size k of offset and the direction dir, where 1 represents the offset to the right and any other number represents the offset to the left.
         Before performing the offset is important to establish the limits, identifying the index pid of the last or first job of p allocated in S and check in accordance with the direction dir if it is possible apply the offset k. The offset to the left is invalid when pid − k < 0, the offset to the right becomes invalid when pid + k ≥ |S|. In both cases of invalid movement, the size of k is decremented until it finds a possible size k or k = 0. Only after checking the validity of offset, all jobs of p are displaced.
         Before performing the offset is important to establish the limits, so it is ascertained index i in relation to dir, if it is possible apply the offset k. The offset to the left is invalid when i − k < 0, the offset to the right becomes invalid when i + k ≥ |S|. In both cases of invalid movement, the size of k is decremented until it finds a possible size for k or k = 0.
         Only after checking the validity of offset, the job j is displaced. This validation does not guarantee that the offset is valid to precedence constraints, so it is necessary to perform a topological sort in S, to sort jobs which are on wrong position.
